I am a tenure-track assistant professor at the Polytechnic University of Bari in Italy. My research focuses on integrating the principles of "trustworthy AI" that prioritize fairness, explainability, and security into recommender systems. I have been lately exploring application of "causal inference" in machine learning systems.
Additionally, I have a track record of experience in "multi-modal information-seeking systems", having worked on integrating content in various multimedia recommender systems such as fashion, video, and music, as well as conversational agents.
In 2018, I earned my Ph.D. with distinction from the Polytechnic University of Milan. I have also served as a visiting researcher at UCSD, and JKU-Linz in Austria. I have also the experience of organizing machine-learning competitions with major industrial partners, such as Twitter, Xing, and Spotify as part of the ACM RecSys Challenge (through 2017-2020). Additionally, I serve as a senior PC, program/track chair at several renowned IR conferences such as SIGIR, TheWebConference, CIKM, and EWAF.

I have contributed to several literature reviews as the lead author, synthesizing the developments in the field of trustworthy AI and multimodal information seeking. My articles have been published in widely-read journals such as ACM CSUR, and were co-authored with major industrial partners. I have had further the honor of contributing book chapter to the third edition of the Recommender Systems Handbook (2022), by F. Ricci, L. Rokach, B. Shapira, regarded as the finest book within the RecSys community.
