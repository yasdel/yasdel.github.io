Yashar Deldjoo is a senior research scientist and a tenure-track Assistant Professor at the Polytechnic University of Bari, Italy. He earned his Ph.D. with distinction in computer science from Politecnico di Milano and an M.Sc. in electrical engineering from the Chalmers University of Technology, Sweden where completed his MSc thesis at the Multimedia Dept. of Ericsson Ressarch. His research focuses on recommender systems, fair machine learning, trustworthy AI, and multimedia analytics.

His recent work explores the integration of Generative AI and Large Language Models (LLMs) into recommender systems. Yashar is co-authoring the forthcoming book **"Recommendation with Generative Models"**, scheduled for publication in 2024, building upon his survey and tutorial presented at SIGKDD 2024. He is organizing the **"1st Workshop on Risks, Opportunities, and Evaluation of Generative Models in Recommender Systems"** at ACM RecSys 2024. In a podcast with Marcel Kurovski at Recspert, he discusses the evolution of generative recommender systems, modeling paradigms, and their evaluation, risks, and harms. Additionally, he is known for his contributions to multi-modal recommender systems, including fashion, textiles, video, and music recommendations, and has contributed chapters to the Recommender Systems Handbook (2021) by F. Ricci, L. Rokach, B. Shapira, the best book in the RecSys community.

In 2023 and 2024, Yashar was recognized among the top 2% of research scientists worldwide according to Stanford University's Ioannidis database.

(1) We are thrilled to announce the release of a major manuscript, the result of extensive collaboration between industry and academia, titled "Recommendation with Generative Models", which is planned for publication in FntIR 2024. This work builds significantly upon our recent survey and tutorial presented at SIGKDD 2024 (Survey-GenRecSys-KDD2024).

(2) Listen to my discussion with Marcel Kurovski at Recspert, "PodCast: Generative Models for Recommender Systems with Yashar Deldjoo". We discuss the evolution of Gen-RecSys, DGMs, modeling paradigms, and scenarios as well as their evaluation, risks, and harms.

(3) We are organizing the "The 1st Workshop on Risks, Opportunities, and Evaluation of Generative Models in Recommender Systems", the main specialized workshop on LLMs and Generative Models AT ACM RecSys 2024 (ROEGEN@RecSys'24). Deadline for submission: August 30, 2024.
